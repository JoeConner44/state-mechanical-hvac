How a Service Agreement Can Save You Money

State Mechanical Team · Cost Savings

More Than Just a Tune-Up

A lot of homeowners think of a service agreement as a nice-to-have — a way to get a checkup for their HVAC system each year. But when you look at the numbers, it’s actually one of the smarter financial decisions you can make as a homeowner.

Preventative Maintenance Catches Problems Early

An annual tune-up gives us a chance to find small problems before they become expensive ones. A refrigerant leak caught early costs a fraction of what a failed compressor costs. A worn capacitor replaced at a tune-up is a $50 part; waiting until the system won’t start in July is an emergency call on a Saturday with a much larger bill.

Studies consistently show that HVAC systems with regular maintenance have fewer breakdowns, lower repair costs over their lifetime, and last significantly longer than neglected systems.

Efficiency Adds Up Every Month

A dirty system works harder to reach your set temperature. Dirty coils, clogged filters, and low refrigerant all reduce efficiency — and you pay for that inefficiency on your power bill every month. A well-maintained system running at peak efficiency can save 5 to 15 percent on energy costs compared to a neglected one.

What’s Included in Our Service Agreement

  • Annual or bi-annual tune-up visits (spring and fall)
  • Full system inspection and cleaning
  • Priority scheduling for repairs — agreement holders move to the front of the line
  • Discounts on parts and labor when repairs are needed
  • System performance tracking over time

Is It Worth It?

For most homeowners, yes. The cost of an agreement is typically recovered in energy savings alone within the first year, before you factor in avoided repairs. And for commercial customers with multiple units, the savings are even more significant.
